I · The Standard
Before anything is designed, the tea has already been decided.

Selection, blending, roasting: in Japanese tea these are not marketing language, they are a discipline refined over decades by people who answer to a standard most producers will never reach. This is the floor everything else is built on.

II · Design as Cultural Translation
Listening comes first. Expression comes only after.

We do not import Japan and set it down somewhere else. We take what is essential in its values and find the form that lets it resonate here, now, for the people actually in front of us.

III · Tea Design
Formats that don't have a name yet.

We do not recreate tea ceremonies. A tradition already exists to do that properly. What we build instead are structured around discovery, wellness, connection, vitality. Whatever the format, the standard underneath doesn't move. Only the shape does.

IV · Other Hands
Innovation has never been a solitary act.

The bowl a tea is served in comes from a ceramicist who has spent years learning what clay can hold, held to the same standard we hold the leaf to. We are not introducing collaboration into this tradition. We are continuing what was already there.

V · In Your Hands

This is tea: made without compromise, shaped by hands beyond our own. Now it is in yours.
