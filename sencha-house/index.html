Some things carry more than form. They carry origin, intention, the discipline behind them, everything it took to make them right. Japanese tea is one of them.

Guided by Ikeda
I · The Standard
BEFORE
ANYTHING
is designed, the tea has already been decided.

Selection, blending, roasting: in Japanese tea these are not marketing language, they are a discipline refined over decades. This is the floor everything else is built on.

II · Design as Cultural Translation
LISTENING
COMES FIRST
expression comes only after.

We do not import Japan and set it down somewhere else. We take what is essential in its values and find the form that lets it resonate here, now, for the people actually in front of us.

III · Tea Design

We do not recreate tea ceremonies. A tradition already exists to do that properly. What we build are formats that don't have a name yet.
